CONTRACT NO. 24000012493 
CITY OF RICHMOND 
GOODS AND SERVICES CONTRACT 
 
This Contract, dated this _______ day of _________________________, 2024 (the “Commencement 
Date”) between the City of Richmond, Virginia (the “City”) and Corvel Enterprise Comp, Inc. (the 
“Contractor”), is binding among and between these parties as of the date of the City’s final signature. 
 
 
WHEREAS, the City has awarded the Contractor this Contract pursuant to Request for Proposals 
No. 230014198 as modified by Addendum 1, dated August 9, 2023, Addendum 2, dated August 15, 2023, 
and Addendum 3 dated August 17, 2023, Addendum 4 dated August 24, 2023, and Addendum 4 dated 
August 28 2023 (the “Request for Proposals No. 230014198”), for Third Party Administration Services 
Workers’ Compensation, General Liability, and Automobile Liability Claims and Loss Control Services. 
 
THEREFORE, in consideration of the Recital set forth above and good and valuable consideration as set 
forth below, the parties agree as follows: 
 
1. Scope of Contract; Agreement to Use of Electronic Signatures. The Contractor shall provide the 
goods and services to the City as set forth in the Contract Documents enumerated in Section 3 below. 
By signing this Contract, the Contractor acknowledges and certifies the Contractor’s agreement to the 
acceptance and use of electronic signatures for purposes of this Contract and any amendments or 
modifications thereto.  The Contractor hereby agrees that electronic signatures shall be treated the 
same as handwritten signatures for the purposes of validity, enforceability, and admissibility.   
 
2. Contract Amount.  The maximum authorized contract amount for this Contract is One Million Seven 
Hundred Eighty-Eight Thousand Five Hundred and Fifty and 00/100 US Dollars ($1,788,550.00). The 
aggregate of all payments by the City under this Contract shall not exceed this amount.  All payments 
shall be as provided in the Contract Documents. 
 
3. Contract Documents.  This Contract shall consist of the following Contract Documents, listed in 
order of precedence from highest to lowest: 
 
A. This Goods and Services Contract between the City and the Contractor. 
B. The Negotiated Modifications to Contract Documents for Contract No.24000012493, attached 
hereto as Attachment A. 
C. The General Terms and Conditions attached to the Request for Proposals. 
D. The Statement of Needs attached to the Request for Proposals. 
E. The Contractor’s Cost Proposal dated June 10, 2024, consisting of six pages, attached hereto as 
Attachment B. 
F. The Contractor’s proposal dated August 30, 2024. 
G. The CareMC License Agreement, consisting of five pages, attached hereto as Attachment C. 
H. The Instructions to Offerors attached to the Request for Proposals. 
 
All of these documents are incorporated herein by reference. 
 
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties hereto on the latest day and year written below have executed 
this Contract.

1. Section 4.2.2 (“Policy Requirements”) of Part III (“General Terms and Conditions”) of Request 
for Proposals No. 230014198 is hereby modified in its entirety to read as follows:

2.11 
Service Level Agreement.  
 
A. 
For each calendar year of the Contract, the Contractor shall meet the determination of 
compensability timeline set forth in section 2.2.1(F) of this Statement of Needs for a 
minimum of 90% of the claims submitted to the Contractor within that calendar year (the 
“Compensability Determination SLA”).  If Contractor fails to meet that Compensability 
Determination SLA for a given calendar year (as determined by the City), then the City 
may reduce the flat annual fee paid to the Contractor for that calendar year by $10,000.00. 
This right shall be in addition to any other rights available to the City under this Contract. 
 
B. 
For each calendar year of the Contract, the Contractor shall meet the required contact 
timeline set forth in section 2.2.1(B) of this Statement of Needs for a minimum of 90% of 
the claims submitted to the Contractor under that section 2.2.1(B) within that calendar year 
(the “Initial Contact SLA”).  For each calendar year of the Contract, the Contractor shall 
meet the required contact timeline set forth in section 2.2.1(C) of this Statement of Needs 
for a minimum of 90% of the notes submitted to the Contractor by treating physicians 
falling under that section 2.2.1(C) within that calendar year (the “Treating Physician 
Contact SLA”). If the Contractor fails to meet either the Initial Contact SLA or the Treating 
Physician Contact SLA, or both, for a given calendar year (as determined by the City) then 
the City may reduce the flat annual fee paid to the Contractor for that calendar year by 
$10,000.00. This right shall be in addition to any other rights available to the City under 
this Contract.  
 
C. 
For each calendar year of the Contract, the Contractor shall meet the required contact 
timeline set forth in section 2.2.2(C)(1) of this Statement of Needs for a minimum of 90% 
of the claims submitted to the Contractor under that section 2.2.2(C)(1) within that calendar 
year (the “Recorded Statement SLA”).  For each calendar year of the Contract, the 
Contractor shall meet the required contact timeline set forth in section 2.2.2(C)(2) of this 
Statement of Needs for a minimum of 90% of the reports submitted to the Contractor under 
that section 2.2.2(C)(2) within that calendar year (the “City Department Contact SLA”). If 
the Contractor fails to meet either the Recorded Statement SLA or the City Department 
Contact SLA, or both, for a given calendar year as determined by the City, then the City 
may reduce the flat annual fee paid to the Contractor for that calendar year by $10,000.  
This right shall be in addition to any other rights available to the City under this Contract.
