# MAP Alignment — A Thriving City Hall

## What is MAP?
Richmond's MAP (Mayor's Action Plan) provides the strategic framework for City priorities. The A Thriving City Hall pillar aligns most directly with themes of white-glove service delivery, fiscal responsibility, government transparency, and empowering staff with tools that serve the mission.

## How City Hall themes connect to Richmond's MAP

### White-Glove Service
The MAP emphasizes treating every resident interaction as if they are a valued customer. The Resident Service Navigation problem directly addresses failure points where residents can't find the right City service, submit the wrong request type, or abandon the process entirely. A tool that helps residents describe their need in plain language and receive the right next step embodies white-glove service at scale.

### Fiscal Responsibility
The Procurement Risk Review problem addresses the risk of missed contract renewals, outdated contract use, and inefficient manual scanning of PDFs and databases. Tools that surface expiring contracts and key procurement details support better fiscal stewardship and reduce the cost of poor procurement decisions.

### Government Transparency
Hackathon tools that visualize City spending, contract data, or procurement decisions contribute to the MAP goal of an open and accountable government. The City Contracts Socrata dataset (xqn7-jvv2) and related federal procurement data (SAM.gov, eVA) are publicly available — tools built on these datasets support transparency without requiring privileged access.

### Staff Empowerment
Both targeted problem statements include a staff dimension. Reducing the volume of misrouted 311 requests frees staff for higher-value work. Easier contract review reduces manual burden on procurement teams. City Hall becomes more effective when staff have better information tools.

## Alignment summary
| City Hall Theme              | MAP Connection                        | Prototype Direction                     |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Resident service navigation  | White-glove service, equity           | Plain-language 311 helper, service finder |
| 311 routing accuracy         | Efficiency, staff capacity            | Category guide, decision tree            |
| Procurement contract review  | Fiscal responsibility                 | Contract expiry dashboard, PDF extractor |
| Spending transparency        | Open government, accountability       | Spending visualizer, contract explorer   |
| Staff decision support       | Staff empowerment, mission alignment  | Triage helper, procurement summary tool  |

## What this pillar is not
- It is not about City economic development, business licensing, or workforce programs (those are Thriving Economy).
- It is not about housing services or tenant navigation (that is a separate pillar).
- It is not a general civic tech wishlist — it is scoped to government operations, service delivery, and procurement.
