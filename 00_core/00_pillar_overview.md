# Pillar Overview — A Thriving City Hall

## Purpose
This repository is a markdown knowledge base for teams working on the A Thriving City Hall pillar in the Richmond Civic Hackathon. It helps teams understand the problem space, review problem statements, explore public data, and shape realistic weekend MVPs.

## Pillar framing
A Thriving City Hall focuses on making Richmond's government more accessible, efficient, and transparent for residents and staff, including:
- helping residents find the right City service or next step in plain language
- reducing miscategorized and misdirected 311 service requests
- helping City procurement staff review contracts across multiple sources
- making government spending and contracting data more visible and useful
- reducing manual staff workload through better information routing
- improving the resident experience on rva.gov and RVA311

## Why this matters
Effective government service delivery drives:
- resident trust and satisfaction with City government
- staff efficiency and reduced workload from misrouted requests
- better procurement decisions and fiscal responsibility
- more equitable access to City services for residents across digital literacy levels
- transparency that enables civic participation and accountability

## Useful prototype directions
Prototypes that often help in this pillar:
- make City service information easier to find and understand in plain language
- help residents identify the right request type before submitting to 311
- make procurement contract data easier to scan and act on
- reveal actionable patterns in spending or contracting for staff and residents
- help staff triage, route, or summarize information faster

## Less useful directions
Prototypes that tend to struggle in a weekend:
- require integration with official City systems (RVA311/AvePoint, EnerGov, Oracle RAPIDS, BizTalk)
- require post-2018 RVA311 data via API (no public API exists)
- make legal or compliance determinations automatically
- replace trained 311, procurement, or licensing staff judgment
- depend on private resident data or internal City databases
- claim to represent the City's official position on a service question
