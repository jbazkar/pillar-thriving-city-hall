User & Stakeholder B1 — Resident Service Seeker Persona

Identify the likely primary user personas for the resident service navigation problem in Richmond. Include first-time City service users, residents dealing with neighborhood quality issues (potholes, dumping, noise), residents seeking permits or licensing information, and non-English-speaking residents.

Richmond demographic context: Richmond has significant multilingual populations; rva.gov supports 36 languages via Google Translate. The city includes neighborhoods with varying digital literacy levels and device access. The RVA311 system handles ~208,216 requests annually across ~50+ categories.

For each persona, describe:
- What they are trying to accomplish
- What they likely already know about City government
- What they don't know (and don't know they don't know)
- What device and access context they're likely using
- What would make the navigation experience successful vs. frustrating for them

Also investigate: what does research say about how residents in similar mid-sized cities actually approach government service navigation — do they call, search the web, use apps, ask neighbors?

Output
- 3–5 personas with goals, knowledge gaps, and device/access context
- Example scenarios and first-step questions for each persona
- Design implications for a plain-language service navigation tool
