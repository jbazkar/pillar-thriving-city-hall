Master Research Framework — A Thriving City Hall (Richmond Civic Hackathon)

This is the master research prompt for the A Thriving City Hall pillar. It covers both targeted problem statements and should be used to establish a research baseline before diving into section-specific prompts.

TARGETED PROBLEM 1: Resident Service Navigation
Richmond residents struggle to identify which department handles their issue, whether a problem requires a service request, and which category to select within the RVA311 system. The City's information architecture is department-centric rather than citizen-centric. Key systems: rva.gov (Drupal 8+/Acquia), RVA311 (AvePoint Citizen Services/Dynamics 365, NO public API, launched June 2018, ~208,216 requests in 2025). Historical Socrata data (vgg4-hjn8) covers SeeClickFix era only (Jan 2014–Aug 2015).

TARGETED PROBLEM 2: Procurement Risk Review
City staff must evaluate contracts from City contracts (Socrata xqn7-jvv2), state VITA contracts (vita.cobblestonesystems.com/public/, web only), federal GSA/SAM.gov contracts (free key required), and eVA Virginia (data.virginia.gov CSV). Key details are buried in PDFs. Staff manually scan documents to find expiration dates, renewal windows, and pricing terms.

Research the following across both problems:
1. What is the documented scale of misrouted 311 requests nationally and in comparable cities?
2. What approaches have worked for plain-language government service navigation (successful tools, architectures, failure modes)?
3. What public data assets are confirmed accessible for Richmond City Hall hackathon work?
4. What are the key technical constraints teams must work around (no post-2018 311 API, Socrata column bug, VITA web-only)?
5. What are the highest-risk failure modes (confidently wrong routing, stale content, hallucinated services)?
6. What national examples of contract management or procurement transparency tools are most relevant?

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- Top 5 evidence-based findings most useful for a hackathon team building this weekend
