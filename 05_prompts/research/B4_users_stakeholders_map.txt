User & Stakeholder B4 — Full Stakeholder Map

Map all stakeholders for both A Thriving City Hall targeted problems. Include residents, City staff, elected officials, and potential implementation partners.

For the resident service navigation problem:
- Residents (first-time users, repeat users, non-English speakers, seniors, mobile-only users)
- 311 staff (Director Pete Briel, routing agents, supervisors)
- rva.gov website team (City IT, Tech Dynamism contractor)
- Department staff who receive misrouted requests
- City Council members (who hear constituent complaints)
- Community organizations that help residents navigate City services

For the procurement problem:
- Procurement officers responsible for contract management
- Department directors who request contracts
- Finance/budget staff overseeing fiscal compliance
- City Auditor (oversight and accountability)
- City Council members (fiscal oversight)
- Vendors and contractors

For each stakeholder group, describe:
- Their interest in the problem (pain/benefit)
- Their power/influence over adoption of a solution
- What they would need to see to support a post-hackathon prototype
- Any publicly documented positions or statements

Output
- Stakeholder map by group with interests and influence level
- Power/interest matrix (who to inform vs. engage vs. champion)
- Implications for post-hackathon adoption strategy
