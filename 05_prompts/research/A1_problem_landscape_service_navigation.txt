Problem Landscape A1 — Resident Service Navigation Reality in Richmond

Investigate the real-world shape of Richmond's resident service navigation problem. Using only verifiable public information and clearly labeled inferences, explain where the difficulty actually occurs when a resident tries to interact with City services: finding the right department, identifying whether a problem requires a service request or information, selecting the correct category in RVA311, or determining whether information on rva.gov is current.

Richmond context: rva.gov is a Drupal 8+ site on Acquia with a Search API using Facets module. RVA311 uses AvePoint Citizen Services on Dynamics 365 (launched June 2018, no public API, ~208,216 requests in 2025, ~50+ request types across 7 department groups). The historical SeeClickFix Socrata dataset (vgg4-hjn8) covers only January 2014–August 2015. The City's information architecture is department-centric rather than citizen-centric.

Investigate: What specifically makes it hard for residents to navigate City services? What is the documented rate of miscategorization or misdirection in similar cities? What categories overlap or cause the most confusion? What happens downstream when a request is miscategorized?

Output
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- Assessment of primary friction points for Richmond residents specifically
