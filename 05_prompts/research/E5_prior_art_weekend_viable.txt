Prior Art E5 — What Can Actually Be Built in a Weekend: Civic Tech Feasibility

Investigate what civic tech tools have actually been built during weekend hackathons (48–72 hours) and what made them successful. Focus specifically on tools relevant to the A Thriving City Hall problem space (service navigation, procurement transparency).

Types of projects to investigate:
1. SUCCESSFUL HACKATHON PROTOTYPES: Projects from Code for America hackathons, National Day of Civic Hacking, or municipal hackathons that addressed service navigation or government transparency
2. SCOPE PATTERNS: What scope and feature set is consistently achievable in 48 hours vs. what consistently fails?
3. DEMO-READY THRESHOLDS: What minimum viable product gets positive judge feedback at civic tech hackathons?
4. TECHNICAL STACK PATTERNS: What technology choices consistently enable fast weekend delivery?

Specific feasibility questions:
- Is a decision-tree 311 helper feasible in 48 hours? What are the most time-consuming parts?
- Is a contract expiry dashboard from Socrata CSV data feasible in 48 hours?
- Is LLM-powered PDF extraction feasible in 48 hours? What are the failure modes?
- Is a rva.gov crawler + search layer feasible in 48 hours?

What do experienced civic hackathon mentors and judges say about common scoping mistakes (too broad, too narrow, wrong user, wrong demo)?

Output
- Feasibility assessment for each major MVP shape in this pillar
- Time estimates for key technical tasks
- Common scoping mistakes and how to avoid them
- Characteristics of successful civic hackathon demos
