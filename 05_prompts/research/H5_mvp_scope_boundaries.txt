MVP Feasibility H5 — Hard Scope Limits for Both Problems

Define the hard scope boundaries for both A Thriving City Hall targeted problems. These are the lines a hackathon team must not cross — either because the data doesn't exist, because crossing them causes civic harm, or because the scope is not buildable in 48 hours.

FOR RESIDENT SERVICE NAVIGATION:

Hard out-of-scope items:
- Live RVA311 data integration (no API)
- Submission of actual service requests through the prototype
- Claiming to represent the City's official position on any service question
- Routing any query involving urgent health/safety issues without an immediate 911/emergency service prompt
- Making eligibility determinations for City programs
- Building a full conversational chatbot that handles all possible resident questions (too broad, too risky)

Hard must-have items:
- Human fallback (link to call 311 or contact department directly)
- Source attribution for every piece of information shown
- Clear "this is not an official City tool" disclosure
- Handling of the "I don't know / please call 311" case

FOR PROCUREMENT REVIEW:

Hard out-of-scope items:
- Making legal compliance determinations
- Recommending specific contract awards
- Accessing internal City financial systems (Oracle RAPIDS, etc.)
- Claiming accuracy of contract dates without a data freshness caveat
- Debarment determinations (SAM.gov data should be surfaced as a flag, not a determination)

Hard must-have items:
- "Advisory only — verify in official systems" disclaimer on all output
- Data source and date attribution for every record shown
- Acknowledgment that the City Contracts dataset may not reflect amendments

Output
- Scope boundary tables for each problem
- Language for explaining scope to judges
- Checklist teams can use to validate their scope before the demo
