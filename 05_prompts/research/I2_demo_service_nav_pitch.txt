Demo Guidance I2 — How to Demo a Service Navigation Tool to Judges

Provide specific guidance on demoing a resident service navigation tool — a 311 helper, rva.gov smart search, or service pathway mapper — to hackathon judges at the Richmond Civic Hackathon.

THE JUDGE AUDIENCE:
What do judges at a civic tech hackathon care about? What questions do they ask? How do they evaluate technical credibility vs. civic impact vs. demo quality?

THE SERVICE NAVIGATION DEMO ARC:
- Opening hook (30 sec): What is the one-sentence problem? What statistic or story anchors it?
- Problem demonstration (45 sec): Show a resident getting lost in rva.gov or picking the wrong 311 category
- Solution in action (90 sec): Show your tool routing the same scenario correctly
- Credibility signal (30 sec): How does your tool handle uncertainty? What is the human fallback?
- Impact statement (30 sec): What does this change for residents and staff?
- Ask/next steps (30 sec): What would it take to deploy this? What's the next step?

SPECIFIC SCENARIOS TO PREPARE:
- Scenario 1 (common, clear): "There's a pothole at the end of my street" → correct DPW routing
- Scenario 2 (common, ambiguous): "There's flooding in my yard after rain" → drainage vs. water main disambiguation
- Scenario 3 (uncertainty handling): "My neighbor's tree fell on the sidewalk" → private vs. public right-of-way distinction
- Scenario 4 (non-English): If you have multilingual support, show it

THINGS JUDGES WILL PROBE:
- "What happens if someone asks something you can't route?"
- "Is this using real Richmond data?"
- "What would it take for the City to actually use this?"
- "How accurate is the routing?"

Output
- Script outline for a 4-minute service navigation demo
- Answers to top 5 judge questions
- Backup plan if the live demo fails
