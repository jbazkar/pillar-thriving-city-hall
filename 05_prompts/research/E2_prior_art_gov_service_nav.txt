Prior Art E2 — Government Service Navigation Tools Nationally

Investigate national prior art for government service navigation tools — tools that help residents find the right government service when they don't know which department or program applies. This is broader than 311 and includes general government information portals, service finders, and AI-assisted navigation tools.

Key examples to investigate:
- USA.gov and USAGov (federal government service finder, including recent AI enhancements)
- UK GOV.UK — the UK digital government transformation, including its service taxonomy and plain-language content standards
- Code for America — what service navigation projects have they built or contributed to?
- State-level examples (California Benefits Screener, benefits.gov, etc.)
- City-level examples beyond 311: Bloomington "Ask Bloomington," New Orleans, Austin
- Smart chatbot deployments: cities that have deployed AI-powered service navigation chatbots and their outcomes

For each tool, document:
- Problem it solves and user type
- Technical approach (static, decision tree, NLP, LLM)
- Data sources used
- Outcome/impact if documented
- Key design decisions (how uncertainty is handled, human fallback, source transparency)

Output
- Inventory of prior art with technical approach and lessons
- Most applicable architecture for a Richmond hackathon prototype
- Design patterns that work vs. patterns to avoid
