Pillar Context — A Thriving City Hall (Richmond Civic Hackathon, March 27–29, 2026)

Provide a concise, factual overview of the A Thriving City Hall problem space in Richmond, Virginia. This pillar focuses on two targeted problems: (1) helping residents find the right City service or next step when they have a need, and (2) helping City staff review procurement contracts across multiple sources more efficiently.

For the resident service problem: Richmond's city information architecture is department-centric rather than citizen-centric. Residents interact with rva.gov (Drupal 8+ on Acquia) and RVA311 (AvePoint Citizen Services on Dynamics 365, no public API, launched June 2018). The historical SeeClickFix Socrata dataset (vgg4-hjn8) covers only January 2014–August 2015. The City website has over 40 department slugs and uses a Drupal Search API with Facets module. Google Translate supports 36 languages.

For the procurement problem: City staff must evaluate contracts from multiple sources — City contracts (Socrata dataset xqn7-jvv2, known API column bug), state VITA contracts (web only, no API at vita.cobblestonesystems.com/public/), federal GSA/SAM.gov contracts (free API key required), and eVA Virginia procurement data (CSV on data.virginia.gov). Key contract details are buried in PDFs.

Investigate what is publicly known about both problems, including comparable cities' approaches, known data limitations, and the gap between what residents expect and what the current systems deliver.
