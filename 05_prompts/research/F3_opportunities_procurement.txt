Opportunities F3 — Best Opportunity Shape for Procurement Contract Review

Investigate the optimal solution shape for the procurement risk review problem in Richmond, given the specific constraints: good public data (City Contracts Socrata CSV), lack of named champion, no pre-staged sample PDFs, and a procurement workflow that is undocumented.

Three competing approaches:
1. CONTRACT EXPIRY DASHBOARD: Ingest the City Contracts CSV (all 9 columns), build a sortable/filterable table or timeline showing contracts by expiration date.
2. PDF CONTRACT EXTRACTOR: Upload a contract PDF → extract key fields (expiration date, renewal window, pricing terms) using LLM or document processing.
3. CROSS-SOURCE CONTRACT FINDER: Query across City Contracts + SAM.gov + eVA simultaneously to show all contracts for a given vendor or commodity type.

For each approach, investigate:
- What is the minimum viable data set?
- What are the most likely failure modes in a 48-hour prototype?
- What would a procurement officer need to see to find it useful?
- How does it handle the "advisory only" requirement (no compliance determination)?
- What prior art exists for each approach?

Also investigate: Which approach is most likely to attract a post-hackathon champion despite the current gap? Would a working contract expiry dashboard be more persuasive to City staff than a PDF extractor or cross-source finder?

What does the procurement transparency literature say about what municipal staff find most valuable in contract management tools?

Output
- Comparative analysis of three approaches
- Recommended approach for the Richmond hackathon context
- Minimum viable feature set for a credible demo
