Top 10 Research Questions — A Thriving City Hall

Answer these 10 key questions that a Richmond Civic Hackathon team working on the A Thriving City Hall pillar most needs answered before building. Focus on Richmond-specific evidence where it exists; use comparable city evidence where Richmond data is unavailable.

1. What is the current list of ~50+ RVA311 request types and their department mappings? Where is this list publicly documented?

2. What is the rva.gov sitemap structure — how many pages exist, are they organized by department, and what structured data fields are available for search indexing?

3. What are the actual 9 columns in the City Contracts Socrata dataset (xqn7-jvv2), including the column that is missing from the API response due to the known bug?

4. What is the free API key registration process for SAM.gov, and what are the rate limits and key data fields available for federal contract queries relevant to Richmond?

5. What is the current URL and schema for the eVA Virginia procurement CSV dataset on data.virginia.gov?

6. How do comparable cities (Boston, NYC, Chicago, SF) structure their 311 service type taxonomies for plain-language resident use?

7. What are the documented failure modes of government service navigation chatbots — specifically cases where wrong routing caused resident harm or eroded trust?

8. What is the AvePoint Citizen Services platform's public documentation — does it confirm the absence of a public API, and what integration capabilities does it officially support?

9. What contract transparency tools built on Socrata or similar open data have been successfully deployed at the city or state level?

10. What digital equity and accessibility considerations are most relevant for Richmond residents using City service navigation tools (language, device, literacy)?

Output
- Answers with URLs for each question
- Clearly mark each answer as: Verified (with source), Inference, or Unknown
- Flag any question where the answer directly affects hackathon feasibility
