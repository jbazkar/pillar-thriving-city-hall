User & Stakeholder B2 — 311 Call Center Agent and Routing Staff

Investigate the role and experience of 311 call center agents and routing staff in Richmond. Pete Briel is the Director of 311 and is identified as a potential champion for the resident service navigation problem. The RVA311 system uses AvePoint Citizen Services on Dynamics 365 and handles ~208,216 requests annually.

What is the daily workflow of a 311 routing agent? What tools do they use to categorize and route requests? What percentage of their time is spent re-routing miscategorized requests submitted online? What types of requests most commonly arrive in the wrong category?

Investigate nationally: What does the 311 workforce literature say about the burden of manual re-routing? What metrics do cities use to measure routing accuracy? Are there published benchmarks for "first-time right" routing rates?

For Richmond specifically: Is there any public reporting on RVA311 staffing, volume, or routing accuracy? What has Pete Briel or the City said publicly about 311 system performance or resident experience?

How would a plain-language service navigator affect the 311 staff workflow — what would they gain, what risks would they face from an inaccurate tool?

Output
- Staff persona description with workflow and pain points
- National benchmarks on routing accuracy and re-routing burden
- Richmond-specific information (with source URLs)
- Implications for tool design: what would staff need to trust a navigation tool?
