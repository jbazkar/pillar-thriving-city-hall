Problem Landscape A4 — Root Causes: Department-Centric Architecture and System Fragmentation

Investigate the root causes behind both A Thriving City Hall problems. The rubric document identifies two structural causes: (1) the City's information architecture is department-centric rather than citizen-centric, and (2) service information is fragmented across multiple systems (rva.gov, RVA311, EnerGov, Oracle RAPIDS, ArcGIS, external portals).

For the department-centric architecture problem: How did this pattern emerge in municipal governments? What are the documented consequences for resident experience? What have successful cities done to reorient around citizen needs rather than department structure?

For the system fragmentation problem: Richmond's technology stack includes Drupal/Acquia (web), AvePoint/Dynamics 365 (311), Microsoft BizTalk (middleware), Trimble Cityworks (DPW work orders), EnerGov/Tyler Technologies (permitting), Oracle RAPIDS (finance/procurement), and ArcGIS (GIS). How do cities manage this fragmentation without replacing all systems? What lightweight integration approaches have worked?

Specifically investigate whether there are known academic or practitioner frameworks for measuring the cost of department-centric vs. citizen-centric service architectures in city governments.
