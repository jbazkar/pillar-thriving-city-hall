Problem Landscape A5 — Government Website UX Failures: National and Richmond Context

Investigate the documented patterns of government website UX failures, focusing specifically on the resident service navigation problem. Richmond's rva.gov uses Drupal 8+ on Acquia with a Facets-based search — a common configuration for mid-sized city websites.

National context: What does the research say about how residents actually search for government services? What are the most common failure modes (wrong search terms, department-centric navigation, outdated content, mobile unfriendliness)? What accessibility and digital literacy barriers are documented?

Richmond context: What is publicly known about rva.gov's usability? Is there any public reporting on resident satisfaction with City website search? Are there any documented complaints or feedback about 311 category confusion?

Drupal-specific context: What are known limitations of Drupal Search API with Facets for citizen-facing search? What improvements have comparable Drupal-based government sites made to search quality?

Plain-language government content: What does the plain-language literature say about the most effective approaches to translating government jargon into resident-friendly service descriptions?
