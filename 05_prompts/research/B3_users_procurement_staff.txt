User & Stakeholder B3 — City Procurement Staff

Investigate the role and experience of City procurement staff who review contracts across multiple sources. Richmond's procurement problem involves staff evaluating City contracts (Socrata xqn7-jvv2), state VITA contracts (vita.cobblestonesystems.com/public/), federal GSA/SAM.gov, and eVA Virginia procurement.

What is the daily workflow of a municipal procurement officer responsible for contract management? What tools do they currently use? How do they track contract expiration dates and renewal windows? How do they compare options across City, state, and federal sources?

Investigate nationally: What does the municipal procurement literature say about the burden of manual contract review? What are the most common types of procurement risk that result from poor contract tracking (missed renewals, continued use of expired contracts, failure to capture state contract savings)?

For Richmond specifically: Is there any public reporting on City procurement processes, staffing, or known procurement failures? Who in the City is responsible for procurement oversight (Director of Finance, Chief Procurement Officer)?

How would a contract expiry dashboard or PDF extractor affect procurement staff workflow? What would staff need to trust a tool that surfaces contract information?

Output
- Procurement staff persona description with workflow and pain points
- National benchmarks on procurement risk from manual processes
- Richmond-specific information (with source URLs)
- Implications for tool design: what would staff need from a decision-support tool?
