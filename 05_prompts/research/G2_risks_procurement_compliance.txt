Risks G2 — Legal and Compliance Risks in Procurement Tools

Investigate the specific legal and compliance risks in building a procurement tool for Richmond City staff. The procurement problem involves contracts with legal and financial implications — a wrong recommendation or misrepresentation could have serious consequences.

What are the legal requirements governing Richmond's procurement decisions?
- What Virginia procurement law applies to Richmond City contracts?
- What are the consequences of procurement decisions that violate these requirements?
- What is the line between "decision support" and "making a procurement determination"?

Risk scenarios to investigate:
1. A dashboard shows a contract has expired, but it has been informally extended — staff relies on the tool and loses the actual contract date
2. An LLM-extracted contract term is wrong (e.g., extracted renewal date is off by one year) — staff misses renewal window
3. A cross-source tool suggests a vendor has no debarment issues, but there's a recent debarment not yet in the data — staff awards a contract to a debarred vendor
4. The tool recommends a state contract as "better value" without knowing all the pricing details — staff follows the recommendation inappropriately

Mitigation requirements:
- What disclaimers are legally sufficient for an "advisory only" tool?
- What verification steps should be required before a staff member acts on tool output?
- What level of data freshness is required before contract dates can be reliably displayed?

Output
- Specific risk scenarios with likelihood and severity assessment
- Legal constraint summary (Virginia procurement law references)
- Required disclaimers and verification workflow
- Language patterns that imply authority (must be avoided)
