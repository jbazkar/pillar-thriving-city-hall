Opportunities F1 — Ranked Solution Spaces for A Thriving City Hall

Rank the possible solution spaces for the A Thriving City Hall pillar based on: (1) evidence of real user need, (2) availability of public data, (3) 48-hour buildability, (4) demo clarity, and (5) post-hackathon adoption potential.

Solution spaces to rank:
A. Plain-Language 311 Helper (decision tree or NLP over ~50 request types)
B. RVA.gov Smart Search (search layer over crawled City website content)
C. Service Pathway Mapper (static/semi-static guide to common resident situations)
D. Contract Expiry Dashboard (Socrata CSV + expiration date filtering)
E. PDF Contract Extractor (LLM extraction of key terms from contract documents)
F. Cross-Source Contract Finder (City + SAM.gov + eVA unified query)
G. Government Transparency Dashboard (spending visualization from multiple public sources)

For each, provide:
- Evidence score: how well documented is the user need?
- Data score: how accessible and complete is the required data?
- Buildability score: how realistic is a 48-hour working demo?
- Demo clarity score: how easy is the value proposition to communicate in 5 minutes?
- Adoption score: how likely is a City champion to continue this post-hackathon?

Richmond-specific context:
- 311 helper is constrained by no post-2018 data API but has a strong potential champion (Pete Briel)
- Contract dashboard has good public data (Socrata CSV) but lacks a named champion
- PDF extraction requires pre-staged sample documents that are not yet available

Output
- Ranked list with scores per dimension
- Top recommendation with rationale
- Second choice recommendation for teams with different skill profiles
