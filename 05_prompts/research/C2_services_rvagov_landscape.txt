Services Landscape C2 — rva.gov Structure, Search, and Content Organization

Investigate the structure and capabilities of rva.gov in detail. The site uses Drupal 8+ on Acquia cloud hosting, developed by Tech Dynamism. Search is implemented via Drupal Search API with Facets module. The search URL pattern is: https://www.rva.gov/search?search={query}&page={n}&f[0]=business_unit:{id}

Sitemap and structure: Does rva.gov publish a sitemap (sitemap.xml)? How many pages are there? How are pages organized — by department, by service, or by topic? What are the 40+ department slug identifiers that appear in the search facets?

Search quality: What does the Facets module provide in terms of search faceting and filtering? How does the search rank results — by recency, relevance, or department? What are known limitations of Drupal Search API for citizen-facing search compared to dedicated search platforms?

Content organization: Is rva.gov organized around departments or around citizen tasks? Is there a "services" section or similar citizen-centric navigation? What service description formats exist on the site?

Translation: Google Translate widget supports 36 languages. What are the documented limitations of this approach for government service content?

Content freshness: Is there a "last updated" date on rva.gov pages? How often is content updated? What is the content governance model?

Structured data: Does rva.gov use schema.org or other structured data markup that would make content easier to index programmatically?

Output
- Facts (with URLs) on rva.gov structure and capabilities
- Practical guidance for a hackathon team building a search layer over rva.gov
- Key limitations and workarounds
