Data Inventory D5 — Data Quality Issues, Gaps, and What's Missing

Investigate the data quality issues and gaps that most directly affect a hackathon team's ability to build credible prototypes for the A Thriving City Hall pillar.

RVA311 data gaps:
- The post-2018 AvePoint data is completely inaccessible publicly. What does this mean for ground-truthing a 311 category helper? Can 2014–2015 SeeClickFix categories be assumed to still be valid?
- How stale is the historical data for understanding current request patterns? What changed between 2015 and 2025 in Richmond's service delivery?

City Contracts data quality:
- What is the known column bug in the SODA API for xqn7-jvv2? Which column is missing?
- What is the completeness rate for expiration dates? How many contracts have null or invalid end dates?
- Are vendor names normalized, or do the same vendor appear under multiple name variations?
- Are contract amounts consistently reported, or are there outliers/anomalies?

rva.gov content quality:
- How often does rva.gov content become outdated? Are there known pages with incorrect or superseded information?
- What percentage of department pages have consistent, machine-readable contact and service information?
- How well does the Google Translate quality hold up for government service terminology?

Cross-source data joining:
- How reliably can vendor names be matched across City Contracts, SAM.gov, and eVA? What are the known normalization challenges?
- How well do NAICS codes align between City, state, and federal procurement data?

Output
- Data quality inventory by source
- Recommended mitigations for each quality issue
- What teams should communicate to judges about data limitations
