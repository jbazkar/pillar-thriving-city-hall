Missing Information Gaps — A Thriving City Hall

Identify the critical information unknowns that a Richmond Civic Hackathon team working on this pillar cannot verify from public sources. For each gap, describe its impact on hackathon feasibility and suggest a workaround.

CRITICAL GAP 1: Post-2018 RVA311 data
The AvePoint Citizen Services platform has no public API and does not publish to Socrata. Teams have no access to: current request volumes by category, current routing logic, category taxonomy as it exists in the live system, or resolution rates by type. What is the impact of this gap on building a 311 navigation tool? What substitutes or workarounds exist?

CRITICAL GAP 2: Pre-staged procurement PDFs
The rubric document notes that "sample procurement PDFs not pre-staged" is a gap for the procurement problem. Without representative contract documents, PDF extraction tools cannot be tested or demoed reliably. What are realistic sources for sample municipal contract PDFs that could substitute for Richmond-specific documents?

CRITICAL GAP 3: rva.gov analytics and content inventory
There is no public data on which rva.gov pages get the most traffic, which searches fail most often, or which departments generate the most resident confusion. What public proxies exist (e.g., 311 call reason data, common search terms) that could stand in for this analytics data?

CRITICAL GAP 4: 311 routing decision logic documentation
The rubric notes "311 routing decision logic undocumented." The decision tree that maps resident issues to categories and departments is not publicly documented. What can be reconstructed from public sources (rva.gov department pages, historical Socrata data, AvePoint documentation)?

CRITICAL GAP 5: VITA contract portal data structure
VITA statewide contracts are accessible via the Cobblestone web portal but have no API. What fields are visible, what search capabilities exist, and how reliably can the data be extracted for a cross-source comparison tool?

Output
- For each gap: impact rating (critical/high/medium), workaround options, and what the team must accept as an assumption
- Summary: what the team CAN confidently build vs. what requires assumptions or proxies
