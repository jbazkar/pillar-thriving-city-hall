Demo Guidance I5 — Framing Language: "This Helps X Do Y, Instead of Z"

Provide specific framing language for A Thriving City Hall demos. The most effective civic tech demo language is concrete, user-centered, and honest — "this helps [specific user] do [specific task] instead of [current painful approach]."

THE FRAMING FORMULA:
"This helps [user] [do specific task] [using specific data/approach], instead of [current approach], without [overclaiming what it does]."

SERVICE NAVIGATION FRAMING:
- Good: "This helps Richmond residents find the right 311 category before they submit a request, instead of guessing from a dropdown of 50 options and potentially getting re-routed."
- Better: "This helps a resident who sees illegal dumping on their street find the correct report type in under 60 seconds, using plain-language descriptions — instead of navigating multiple rva.gov department pages or picking the wrong category and waiting to be re-routed."
- Bad (overclaims): "This fixes Richmond's 311 system and routes all requests automatically."
- Bad (underdescribes): "This is a chatbot that answers City questions."

PROCUREMENT FRAMING:
- Good: "This helps City procurement staff see which contracts are expiring in the next 60–90 days, instead of manually sorting a spreadsheet."
- Better: "This turns Richmond's publicly available contract registry into an early-warning dashboard — so procurement staff can see at a glance which contracts need attention this month, instead of downloading a CSV and building pivot tables."
- Bad (overclaims): "This automates procurement risk management."
- Bad (underdescribes): "This shows contract data."

FRAMING DO'S AND DON'TS:
- DO name the specific user, task, and current pain
- DO name the data source
- DO include what the tool explicitly does not do
- DON'T claim to replace staff judgment
- DON'T imply official City status
- DON'T use vague impact language ("improves service," "saves time")

Output
- 5–6 tested framing sentences for each City Hall problem type
- Common framing mistakes and how to correct them
- Opening sentence options for the demo pitch
