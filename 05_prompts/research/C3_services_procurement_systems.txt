Services Landscape C3 — City Contract Systems: OpenGov, Oracle RAPIDS, VITA, GSA, SAM

Investigate the procurement systems landscape relevant to Richmond's contract management. Staff use multiple sources to evaluate contracts.

CITY SYSTEMS:
- Oracle RAPIDS: Richmond's financial and procurement management system. What is Oracle RAPIDS? Is it an off-the-shelf Oracle product or a Richmond-specific implementation? Is there any public documentation on its capabilities or data model?
- City Contracts Socrata (xqn7-jvv2): The public-facing representation. What is the full 9-column schema? What is the date range of records? What is the known API column bug?

STATE SYSTEMS:
- VITA (vita.cobblestonesystems.com/public/): Virginia IT Agency statewide contracts. What categories of contracts are listed? What fields are visible in the public portal? How current is the data?
- eVA (data.virginia.gov): Virginia electronic procurement CSV. What is the current dataset URL? What are the key fields?

FEDERAL SYSTEMS:
- SAM.gov: What is the free API key registration process? What are the key endpoints for contract data and debarment checks? What are the rate limits?
- GSA Schedules: How do federal supply schedules work, and how would a city procurement officer use them?
- USASpending.gov: What data is available, what is the API structure, and how granular is the Richmond-specific data?

Output
- Facts (with URLs) on each system
- Access method and key fields for each
- Practical guidance for a hackathon team building a cross-source contract tool
