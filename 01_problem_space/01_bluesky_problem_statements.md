# Blue Sky Problem Statements — A Thriving City Hall

These broader framings are useful for inspiration and long-term thinking. Teams should narrow them before building — a blue sky statement alone is not a weekend-buildable project.

---

## Unified Digital Front Door — 20/27 (Strong) ★ Recommended for narrowing

How might we create a unified digital front door for Richmond residents so that any question about City services — regardless of which department handles it — is answered quickly, correctly, and in plain language, reducing the need to know in advance how the City is organized?

Notes: Strong concept with clear civic value. The challenge is scope — a true "unified front door" implies integration across many departments and systems. For the hackathon, narrow to a single service domain (e.g., property and neighborhood issues, permits, 311 requests) with a clear demo path. Avoid claiming integration with official City systems.

---

## Better Communication Between City and Residents — 19/27 (Needs work)

How might we use technology to improve two-way communication between Richmond's City government and its residents so that residents feel heard, informed, and confident that their service requests are being addressed?

Notes: Lacks constraints and a clear stakeholder. Two-way communication could mean anything from notifications about 311 request status to council meeting summaries to neighborhood alerts. Teams should narrow to one communication channel and one user need. Be skeptical of tools that require integration with official City notification systems.

---

## Making Fiscal Responsibility More Visible — 21/27 (Strong)

How might we use technology to make Richmond's fiscal decisions, contract awards, and budget spending more visible and understandable to residents and oversight bodies so that public accountability is strengthened and trust in City government is improved?

Notes: Strong data foundation exists. The City Contracts Socrata dataset (xqn7-jvv2), SAM.gov, eVA, and related procurement data are publicly available. A transparency dashboard or contract explorer built on these sources could be a compelling weekend project. Avoid claiming the tool represents official City financial reporting.
